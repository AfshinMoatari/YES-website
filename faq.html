    • How is teammates different from conventional service companies?

      We are very different in many ways. The main difference is that our vision with the company is not making profits for ourselves. It’s about making a social difference in Denmark but also around the world through various projects.

    • Which areas can Teammates assist in fulfilling my labor requirements?

      Restaurants, bars, warehouses and event management are just a few of the areas where we have successfully facilitated labour market integration.

    • How do I hire an employee through teammates?

      The first step for any organization or company wishing to hire through teammates is to write an email /call us with the details of the specific needs at team@teammates.nu/42444540.

    • What types of employees can I hire through Teammates?

      Our vision is to facilitate the integration of refugees, asylum seekers and migrants through active participation in the Danish labor market. Therefore, the teammates you can hire come from one of the groups mentioned above. Further, we will always prioritize teammates that are in most need of our help.

    • How can teammates.nu guarantee that their teammates are qualified?

      We guarantee the qualifications of our teammates by conducting several interviews with candidates in our database. Because we understand that some newcomers to Denmark might not have the industry experience, we will provide any training sessions needed prior to them filling a job position.

    • What is the minimum monthly number of hours I should offer to hire a teammate?

      As a rule of thumb, we require at least 60 hours per month for our teammates to ensure a part-time position, also the service fee from the 60 hours gives us the ability to sponsor a single child for a month and pay off all required costs. We do understand that some positions equate to less than 60 hours a month and in these cases we occasionally make exceptions.

    • How long can I have an employee hired through teammates?

      There is no time limit as to how long you can have a teammate at your company. However, the minimum duration for a contract is six months, where the first month is a trial period to ensure we are a perfect partnership.

    • What is the Teammate ”service guarantee”?

      The service guarantee is us promising that the position you need filling will always have a teammate there. Even if your appointed teammate is sick. We therefore promise to find a temporary replacement that is equally qualified. The service guarantee also ensures that if you need an extra teammate with short notice we can fulfill this requirement.

    • Does teammates provide last minute service?

      Yes. We maintain a group of select teammates that can step in with three hours’ notice depending on the location of your workplace. If you use this part of our service, keep in mind that the wages are subject to a 20% increase because to employee is “on call”.

    • What would happen if I do not like the teammate allocated to me?

      If you have hired a teammate through us and things don’t work out, we will make sure to find you an alternative as soon as the problem is brought to our attention.

    • What is teammates legal status?

      We are an Iværksætterselskab (IVS) also called Entrepreneurial limited company. Under the IVS legislation our company category is a Social Economic Business.

    • How does teammates maintain its legal responsibility with business partners and its own employees?

      Teammates uses a service and employment contract that is based on Danish law, this ensures that all the legal requirements are fulfilled.

    • Are teammates employees covered by work insurance?

      Under Danish law the responsibility of work insurance falls to the company that reaps the benefits of the labor. As such it is the companies where the teammate works that must insure the worker. We will make sure that each teammate has adequate insurance wherever they work. Teammates does have a liability insurance in case of damages to property where the teammate is working. If you have any questions concerning insurance for your teammate. Email us at jhdg@yescph.co

    • How can teammates guarantee all their members hold a legal work permit?

      We are working together with all the government agencies to cross check our candidate’s passport, residence card, civil registration card and police certificate and you will receive all the verified copies.

    • How is teammates funded?

      The founding of Teammates was provided by Riad Arefin, the founder. Our plan for the future is to support our daily operation through the service fee.

    • Why do Teammates charge a service fee for its service?

      The service fee that we charge goes into maintaining and building the company. As much as 30% of the service is spent on various socially developing projects Nationally and internationally.

    • How does teammates spend the service fee, that they receive from their customers?

      We spend an estimated 25% on social development. This covers child sponsorship and supporting social projects. We spend 15% on legal expenses, lawyer, accountant, insurance etc. We spend 55% on maintaining teammates as well as building upon the existing organizational structure. This leaves 5 % for unexpected costs and can be added to the “giveback” that we provide companies that hire through us for an extended period of time.

    • How does teammates ensure transparency in their transactions and therefore maintain the non-profit vision?

      We maintain what is called an “Open Book Accounting” policy. This means that other people have the option, after requesting it, to view our account including transactions. Through this transparency our hope is that it eliminates any doubt concerning our non-profit vision.

    • How does using teammates services make my business socially responsible?

      This is because you contribute to ensuring fair and safe employment for asylum seekers, refugees and newcomers to Denmark. You also make a financial contribution to social development projects in developing countries.

    • How do teammates recognize my social contribution?

      To recognize the companies and organizations we work with, we provide a CSR certificate, promote your contribution through online marketing campaigns.

    • Who are the children that teammates provide financial support for?

      All the children are financed through Jaago, a nonprofit organization, based in Bangladesh. Jaago ensures that the children are from most marginalized families that cannot support the education of their children. To find out more visit: jaago.com.bd

    • Can I choose a specific child who I want to sponsor?

      No, we allow our partner organization Jaago to choose which child has the most need.

    • What kind of other social development project are available to support by using the surplus from service fee?

      We have plan to support development project that supports child education and healthcare, women empowerment, businesses that improve local conditions and healthy living for the most vulnerable.

    • How can teammates guaranty that social contributions are spent on children’s needs?

      We can provide any contributor with the documentation we receive. Furthermore, we can provide any contributor with a quarterly update on the child/children being sponsored.

    • Who are the Teammates?

      Teammates is a social enterprise that aims to help immigrants integrate into Danish society through employment. This is done by respectfully building bridges between immigrants and businesses in need of skilled labor. We guarantee reliable, flexible, high quality staff members to perform low skilled entry level jobs. We are not profit driven, all profits go directly into fighting social issues in Denmark and all over the world.

      If you want to support teammates by spreading the word of what we are doing, you can like our pages on social media. It can also be as easy as simply telling friends and family about us. We always appreciate people assisting us in making a difference.
