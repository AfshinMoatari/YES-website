  • Meet & TALK

    It’s always great to meet each other over a cup of coffee. We believe the main goal for any business is to solve a problem. Once we get to know you over coffee we get to know about the great work you do on a daily basis we can figure out how we can help you solve your problems.

    scope of work

    Through the meet & talk we get the opportunity to know exactly what it is that we can do to make your days easier. Our goal is to design a service that caters to your exact needs.

    specifications

    Once we agree that we are a fit for each other, we will make our partnership official and sign a contract. Then we will find the right teammate(s), materials and instrument to ensure quality.

    work

    Now that all of the initial tasks are out of the way, it’s time to start working. Based of the task list that was agreed upon, we will begin doing the tasks.

    revisions

    The first month of us working is a trial month where we make sure that the fit is good. During this month, we will also revise the task list as well as the estimated working hours so that it matches your actual needs.

    finalization

    After having finalized the hours and working tasks, we still continuously work to improve. We understand that businesses are often dynamic and we are happy to adapt to the change to meet your requirement whenever needed.

    impact

    Now that the work process has been established we encourage you to interact with your teammate(s) and make them feel welcome. Furthermore, this is the stage where we will provide you with material regarding your child sponsorship and after 6 months we will engage you with options for the giveback.

    This is how we solve your problem and you solve social problems

  • The cost of working with us falls into three di erent categories: employee wage, service fee and material cost. We follow standardized processes to ensure full transparency that equally all parties involved.

    MATERIAL COST

    There are two options for supplying materials when you work with us:

    You order the materials we use to maintain your facility. We will establish a process in which we inform you when we are running out of materials. If choosing this option, bear in mind that we insist on working with “green” materials that leave the smallest footprint on the environment.

    We handle the process of ordering materials. Using responsible suppliers, we will set up an account in your name and make a list of materials that we need to handle your facility. We will make sure to use green materials that are good to the environment. After the initial setup, we will continuously maintain the supply and all you do is sign off on the bills that you receive directly from the suppliers.

  • PAYMENT PROCESS

    Because our wish is to secure transparency there are two options for payment.

    You pay your teammates’ salary directly to that person and our service fee separately to us. In this way, you always know exactly what the employee is being paid.

    You pay the salary and the service fee to us and we handle the salaries. We can provide you with a copy of the pay slip every month on request.

    We calculate working months from the first day of every month to the last. This means that you will receive an invoice the 1st of every month. You will have 14 days to pay the invoice, meaning that we should receive your payment on the 15th at the latest. This enables us to pay salaries on the 20th of every month. Should you choose to pay via option 1, you will be required to pay the salaries on the same day.

    INSURANCE

    While YES always does our utmost to make sure that everything is done in a safe way, so that both the employees and the areas they work in are safe, things can go wrong. Therefore, there are two types of insurance that we work with.

    Work injury insurance

    This insurance covers injuries to the employee in the workplace. This is the part of the insurance that we ask the companies that we work with to handle. The nature of the work we do involves sustainable integration. As such, we encourage companies to interact with their teammates, which involves taking responsibility for their wellbeing and making sure that the areas where they work are safe. Furthermore, according to the Danish Business Authority the recipient company is responsible for providing the work injury insurance for the teammate working with them.

    Liability Insurance

    This insurance covers damage done by the teammate at the recipient company. It covers damages up to 10 million DKK. While we have not been forced to utilize this insurance, and hope that we never will, we understand that mistakes can be made. Therefore, this is our way of easing the minds of companies we work with in terms of potential mistakes made by teammates in their process of learning their way around the new place of work.
